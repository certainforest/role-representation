## Natural Task
In the natural prompt example, the key is for the model to realize the sentence “I think the other part to mention…” belongs to Joe.

The transcript involves three participants:
Jasmine (reporter) asking about tariffs and job creation
Richard (economist) arguing that structural factors (retaliation, higher costs, export losses, lack of domestic capacity) limit tariffs’ effectiveness
Joe (editor) adding that policy uncertainty discourages firms from making long-term investments or hiring.

Questions
Does Joe think policy uncertainty is the main reason tariffs may fail to create jobs?
Does Richard think policy uncertainty is the main reason tariffs may fail to create jobs?
Ground truth:
Joe → Yes (uncertainty is his primary explanation)
Richard → No (he emphasizes structural economic factors)

The model successfully:

But it failed at:

❌ Evaluating “main reason” from a speaker-local perspective

The task reveals that even when LLMs correctly track who said what, they often fail to preserve agent-specific beliefs and causal priorities, instead collapsing multi-agent discourse into a blended narrative.
